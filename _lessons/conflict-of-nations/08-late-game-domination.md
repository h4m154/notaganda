## The Late Game Phase

The late game is where games are won or lost. Remaining players are typically experienced and well-established. Victory requires careful planning and decisive action.

## Strategic Overview

### Assessing Your Position

In the late game, evaluate:

- Your territory compared to remaining players
- Resource production and reserves
- Military strength and composition
- Alliance relationships and obligations
- Victory condition progress

### Identifying Win Conditions

Determine your path to victory:

- **Conquest** - Control required territory/points
- **Coalition** - Win as part of an alliance
- **Economic** - Outlast opponents through attrition
- **Elimination** - Remove all competitors

## Advanced Weapons

### Researching End-Game Tech

Late game unlocks powerful options:

- Advanced aircraft and missiles
- Nuclear weapons
- Theater missile defense
- Top-tier naval units

### Using Strategic Weapons

When deploying powerful weapons:

- Target high-value strategic objectives
- Consider diplomatic consequences
- Balance destruction with occupation needs
- Protect your own assets from retaliation

## Coalition Warfare

### Coordinating Large Operations

With allies, plan together:

- Define clear objectives
- Assign roles and responsibilities
- Coordinate timing of attacks
- Share intelligence and resources

### Managing Alliance Politics

Late game alliances face pressure:

- Victory sharing disputes
- Territory distribution
- Betrayal risks
- Different strategic priorities

## Closing Out Games

### Maintaining Momentum

Don't let victory slip away:

- Keep pressure on remaining opponents
- Prevent enemy coalitions from forming
- Secure victory conditions systematically
- Don't overextend or become complacent

### Dealing with Desperate Opponents

Cornered players may:

- Make suicide attacks
- Use scorched earth tactics
- Seek last-minute alliances
- Deploy nuclear weapons

### Securing Victory

Final steps to win:

1. Achieve required victory conditions
2. Eliminate or neutralize remaining threats
3. Maintain defensive posture until game ends
4. Celebrate your hard-earned victory!

## Lessons Learned

### Post-Game Analysis

After each game, review:

- What worked well
- What could be improved
- Key decision points
- Strategies to try next time
